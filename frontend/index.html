⛓️ Consensus Algorithm — Proof of Authority (PoA)

This system runs on a Proof of Authority (PoA) consensus network (Hardhat local / deployable to Besu or Quorum for production). PoA is the correct choice for a permissioned institutional environment because: validators are known, trusted entities (universities, government bodies) rather than anonymous miners; it provides instant transaction finality with no probabilistic confirmation delays; it consumes no energy unlike Proof of Work; and it requires no token economics — institutions interact at near-zero gas cost on a private chain. Unlike Proof of Stake, PoA does not require validators to lock up capital, making it suitable for academic and government institutions that operate on fixed IT budgets. The immutability guarantee is preserved: once a certificate hash is written to the ledger by an authorized institution wallet, no validator can alter it without consensus of all known authorities — making fraud immediately detectable via hash mismatch.
